Knox Guard — How It Works

A step-by-step guide to device registration, enrollment, and remote control on AIDOO TECH hire-purchase phones.

Knox Guard is Samsung's remote device management system. AIDOO TECH uses it to lock and unlock financed phones when customers miss payments or settle their account. This guide explains the four-stage flow from receiving a new phone to controlling it remotely.

🪜 Step-by-Step
1

Upload — Register the IMEI with Samsung

When a new Samsung device arrives in inventory, its IMEI is sent to Samsung's Devices API. This tells Samsung the phone belongs to the AIDOO TECH Knox tenant.

This happens automatically when the device is added to inventory (if the Knox upload checkbox is ticked), or manually from the Knox Devices page.

Inventory → Knox Upload Status = UPLOADED
2

Enroll — Link the Device to a Contract

When a hire-purchase contract is created, the system automatically enrolls the device. Enrollment links the phone to the contract and assigns an approveId (the contract number) that Samsung uses to identify it.

A ManagedDevice record is created in the system. The device appears in the Managed Devices table.

Auto on contract creation/approval ManagedDevice record created
3

Approve — Knox Guard App Connects

The customer powers on the phone. The Knox Guard app (pre-installed on Samsung devices) connects to Samsung's servers and the device moves from Accepted to Enrolled on the portal.

This step is automatic — no admin action needed. The cron job checks every 5 minutes and updates the local record to ACTIVE once the portal confirms enrollment.

Automatic — phone must be online enrollmentStatus = ACTIVE
4

Lock / Unlock — Remote Device Control

Once a device is ACTIVE, the system can send lock and unlock commands directly to Samsung. Samsung pushes the command to the phone the next time it connects to the internet.

  • Auto-lock: contract overdue ≥ 3 days → cron locks the device
  • Auto-unlock: payment received → overdue amount = 0 → device unlocked
  • Manual lock/unlock: admin button on Knox Devices or Inventory page
🔒 actualState = LOCKED / UNLOCKED
📵 What the Customer Sees When Locked

The Knox Guard lock screen replaces the normal phone screen with:

The customer can open the payment app or dial the USSD directly from the lock screen.

🏷️ Status Meanings

Accepted

IMEI uploaded to Samsung but Knox Guard app hasn't connected yet. Phone needs to go online.

Enrolled / ACTIVE

Knox Guard app connected. Device is ready to receive lock/unlock commands.

Locked

Lock command sent and confirmed. Customer's phone shows the Knox lock screen.

APPROVAL_QUEUED

Enrolled locally but Samsung hasn't confirmed yet. Waiting for phone to come online.

PENDING

A command (lock/unlock) has been sent to Samsung and is waiting to be delivered to the phone.

FAILED / DEVICE_DUPLICATE

Upload failed. If DEVICE_DUPLICATE, the device is already on Samsung — use Resync to fix.

🛠️ Admin Actions

🔒 Lock

Sends a lock command to Samsung. Phone locks on next internet connection. Message and support number shown on screen.

🔓 Unlock

Sends an unlock command. Retries up to 3 times automatically. If it fails, the cron retries every 5 minutes.

✅ Approve

Manually triggers Knox Guard approval. Usually automatic — use only if the device is stuck in APPROVAL_QUEUED.

⚙️ Evaluate

Re-runs the overdue policy for the contract right now — locks if overdue, unlocks if paid, without waiting for cron.

🔄 The Refresh / Sync Button

Clicking Refresh on the Knox Devices page does three things in one shot:

  1. Marks uploads — any device visible on the Samsung portal but showing FAILED locally is corrected to UPLOADED.
  2. Auto-enrolls — devices on the portal with a contract but no ManagedDevice record are enrolled automatically.
  3. Syncs states — devices that are Enrolled on the portal but APPROVAL_QUEUED locally are promoted to ACTIVE.
⚠️
DEVICE_STATE_INVALID error? This means the Knox Guard app on the phone has not yet connected to Samsung's servers. The phone needs to be powered on with an internet connection. Once it connects, all pending commands (lock/unlock/approve) will be delivered automatically.
❓ Common Questions
Why is my device showing UPLOADED but not in Managed Devices?

Uploaded means the IMEI is registered with Samsung. Managed Devices only shows devices that have been enrolled (linked to a contract) AND are ACTIVE (Knox Guard app connected). Click Refresh on the Knox Devices page to auto-enroll and sync.

I sent a lock command but the phone is still unlocked. Why?

The lock command was sent to Samsung but the phone hasn't received it yet. This happens when the phone is offline or the Knox Guard app isn't running. Once the phone connects to the internet, it will lock automatically. Check the portal status — if it shows Enrolled the command will arrive shortly.

What is DEVICE_DUPLICATE?

This means the device IMEI was uploaded to Samsung more than once. The device IS registered — Samsung just rejected the second attempt. Click the Resync button next to the device in the Knox Upload Status table. The system will check the portal and update the local record to UPLOADED automatically.

Can I lock a device that has no contract yet?

Yes. From the Inventory page, use the lock button on the device row. The message shown on the phone will be: "This Phone is locked Awaiting Contract, Contact AidooTech Manager." The device must be enrolled (ACTIVE) on the Knox portal first.

The payment app button on the lock screen doesn't open. Why?

The app whitelist is configured in the Samsung Knox Guard portal under Profiles → Allowed Applications. Make sure com.aidootech.customer is added with label AIDOO TECH and the correct app signature. This is a portal setting, not an API call. Also note: older Android versions (below Android 13) may not support lock screen app launching.

How does the automatic lock/unlock work?

A background job runs every 5 minutes. It checks every active enrolled device:

• If overdue amount > 0 and days overdue ≥ 3 → lock the device.

• If overdue amount = 0 and device is locked → unlock the device.

• Before locking, a blink warning is sent at day 1–2 overdue.

Payments trigger an immediate re-evaluation, so a customer who pays will be unlocked within seconds — not at the next cron cycle.

What does the support number on the lock screen do?

Tapping it dials *713*9572# (the AIDOO TECH payment USSD) directly from the lock screen. The customer can make a payment without needing to unlock the phone first. This is set in Knox Guard Settings → Support Phone.
